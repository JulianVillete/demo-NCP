Drug Study

Drug Name Classification Mechanism of Action Indication Dosage & Route Nursing Responsibilities
Omeprazole Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI) Suppresses gastric acid secretion by inhibiting hydrogen-potassium ATPase enzyme system in gastric parietal cells Gastric protection, prevention of stress ulcers, GERD 20-40 mg IV/PO once daily • Assess for abdominal pain and GI bleeding
• Monitor for diarrhea and headache
• Administer before meals
• Monitor magnesium levels with long-term use
Ceftriaxone Third-generation Cephalosporin Antibiotic Inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding to penicillin-binding proteins, leading to cell lysis and death Community Acquired Pneumonia, bacterial infections 1-2 g IV every 12-24 hours • Obtain culture before first dose
• Monitor for allergic reactions
• Assess for diarrhea (C. diff risk)
• Protect from light
• Check compatibility with calcium-containing solutions
Azithromycin Macrolide Antibiotic Binds to 50S ribosomal subunit, inhibiting bacterial protein synthesis; bacteriostatic Atypical pneumonia, adjunct therapy for CAP 500 mg IV/PO on day 1, then 250 mg daily • Monitor for QT prolongation and arrhythmias
• Assess liver function
• Watch for GI upset
• Complete full course of therapy
Fluimucil (Acetylcysteine) Mucolytic Agent Breaks disulfide bonds in mucus, reducing viscosity and facilitating expectoration Thick bronchial secretions, mucus clearance in pneumonia 200 mg PO/IV 2-3 times daily or 3-5 mL via nebulizer • Monitor for bronchospasm during nebulization
• Ensure adequate hydration
• Assess sputum characteristics
• Administer with bronchodilator if bronchospasm occurs
Salbutamol (Albuterol) Short-acting Beta-2 Agonist (SABA) Stimulates beta-2 adrenergic receptors in bronchial smooth muscle, causing bronchodilation Bronchospasm, wheezing, difficulty of breathing 2.5 mg nebulization every 4-6 hours or 1-2 puffs inhaler • Assess breath sounds before and after
• Monitor heart rate (tachycardia risk)
• Teach proper inhaler technique
• Watch for tremors and nervousness
• Monitor potassium levels
Sinicod Forte Cough Suppressant/Expectorant Combination Contains codeine (antitussive) and guaifenesin (expectorant) to suppress cough reflex and thin secretions Persistent cough, particularly at night 1 tablet PO every 6-8 hours • Monitor respiratory rate and depth
• Assess for drowsiness and sedation
• Avoid alcohol and CNS depressants
• Monitor for constipation
• Use cautiously in elderly
